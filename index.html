About Me

Hi, I'm Haopeng Deng, a recent graduate of Guangzhou Maritime University, and currently a summer researcher at MIT. My work spans Physics-informed Learning, Surrogate Bayesian Optimization, and Mixed-Autonomy Dynamics. Ultimately, I hope to build more human-centric and sustainable mobility systems, drawing from traffic engineering, data science, control theory, and beyond. Research Experiences

Guangdong Provincial Special Fund for Science and Technology Innovation Strategy

Cooperative Game-Based Network Traffic Assignment Mechanisms for Enhancing Urban Transportation Efficiency 07/2024-04/2026

I lead a project on cooperative game-based traffic assignment mechanisms for urban transportation efficiency. I developed a unified Dynamic Traffic Assignment model that decouples CAV adoption into two dimensions—Automation Level and Connectivity Level—and positions the system along a continuous spectrum between user equilibrium and system optimum within this AL-CL space. I also proposed the AMPAS algorithm to efficiently solve DUE, DSO, and UE-SO mixed equilibria, achieving 27.2% faster convergence than gradient projection on medium-scale networks. Additionally, I authored a systematic review on mixed-traffic DTA models, synthesizing findings from over 120 studies on equilibrium evolution, algorithms, and research trends. I also coordinate task delegation, track milestones, and mentor junior researchers.

Guangdong Provincial College Student Innovation and Entrepreneurship Training Program

Intelligent Recognition of Urban Traffic Equilibrium States under Air-Ground Collaborative Monitoring 11/2024-02/2026

As a core member, I contributed to developing an intelligent system for recognizing urban traffic equilibrium states, integrating data from roadside sensors, UAVs, and probe vehicles. My team aimed to construct high-dimensional traffic flow tensors and explore data loss structures—including element-wise, fiber-wise, and mixed loss—to improve data recovery algorithms. I developed an adaptive truncated Schatten norm-based model (LRTC-ATSN) for recovering traffic data with complex missing patterns, improving MAPE by 10.6% and RMSE by 6.1%. I also proposed SpinFlow, a physics-informed model driven by a latent spin field to capture competitive equilibrium among traffic phases, combined with an Expectation-Maximization algorithm to infer synchronized flow emergence from aerial trajectory data.

High-Quality Development Project for Hundred Counties, Thousand Towns, Myriad Villages

Investigation and Research on Traffic Safety and Planning in Kaiping City 07/2024

I led a team investigating traffic safety and planning in Kaiping City, working with the Kaiping Municipal Transportation Bureau to analyze accident and violation data and identify crash hotspots and hazard types. My team conducted on-site surveys, interviews, and drone photography to gather data on traffic flow, vehicle management, pedestrian movement, and infrastructure. I applied SPSS and LLM tools to analyze the survey and interview data, identifying recurring issues and proposing solutions that were later adopted by the Municipal Transportation Bureau. I also authored two papers from this project: one using Structural Equation Modeling to examine the relationship between traffic safety behavior and attitudes, and another assessing Kaiping's traffic governance through Grey Relational Analysis and Fuzzy Evaluation.
